Requiem of Shadows

Rules & Changes


Available & Required Classes:

Required Classes:

  • Fighter - LVL 3, No Specialization, but expertise in many weapons.
  • Bard - LVL 2, Normal Bard Requirements.
  • Cleric - LVL 1, Normal Cleric Requirements.

Available Classes:

  • Fighter - All but required start at LVL 1. Normal Fighter Requirements.
  • Paladin - Maximum of 1. Normal Paladin Requirements.
  • Ranger - Maximum of 1. Normal Ranger Requirements.
  • Dark Knight - Maximum of 1. Normal Dark Knight Requirements.
  • Wizard - NOT AVAILABLE.
  • Sorcerer - Maximum of 1. Limited "Combat Spells".
  • Cleric - Maximum of 2 including required. Clerics must share the same wisdom stat.
  • Druid - NOT AVAILABLE.
  • Shaman - Mostly Unavailable. May be allowed.
  • Thief - Maximum 2 Rogues in party. Normal Thief Requirements.
  • Bard - Maximum of 1. Normal Bard Requirements.
  • Assassin - Maximum of 1. Normal Assassin Requirements.
  • MULTICLASSING - Available in discussed scenarios - Talk to DM.

Ability Score Changes & Requirements:

Global changes:

  • Strength - No Natural 18s or higher in strength - too much dmg on one person.
  • Dexterity - No Change.
  • Constitution - No Change.
  • Intelligence - No Change.
  • Wisdom - No Change, High Wisdom may be preferred.
  • Charisma - Only 1 person may dump charisma.
  • Perception - Not eligible to be dumpstat. Not eligible to be highest stat - in these cases stats may be changed around.

Abilities for Required Classes

  • Required Fighter - High Wisdom and >10 Charisma Preferred.
  • Required Bard - No Change.
  • Required Cleric - Stats based on Deity.

Abilities for Available Classes

  • Normal Requirements.

*Dark Knight*:

Description:  

A Dark Knight is a Warrior who has gained power from some form of corrupt/dark/mysterious power. Note: Even though their powers may come from corrupt forces, a dark knight is not required to be evil and may be any alignment aside from Neutral Good and Lawful Good.

Ability Requirements:

                A Dark Knight must have the following ability scores:

Strength: 13

Constitution: 13

Intelligence: 13

      Prime Requisites:

Strength: 16

Intelligence: 16

Alignment:

                Any aside from Neutral Good and Lawful Good

Armor:

                Any armor, can only cast in armor that weighs equal or less than chainmail

Weapon Profs:

May become proficient in any weapon and in One-handed and two - handed weapon fight styles. May specialize in a weapon.

Non-Weapon Profs:

                    Learns NWP as a Warrior

      Class Features:

HD: 1d8/lvl -> +3 HP for every lvl beyond lvl 9

Thac0 Scaling: As Warrior (+1 to hit per level)

Saves: As Warrior with a +2 bonus to Saving Throws vs Spell

The Dark Knight receives a few abilities from the force it treats with:

  1. A Dark knight’s abilities are received through the accumulation of Vices. These Vices typically have forms of meaning to the Dark Knight. The Following are examples of Vices:
    • A Part of a monster that has been slain by the Dark Knight himself (I.e. Killng blow). The stronger the monster, the stronger the vice.
    • A considerable form of wealth. This may be a large form of coin ~100 GP per vice, or an item of great or magical value.
    • A sacrifice of a powerful creature or one that has wronged the Dark Knight greatly.
    • Finally, the fastest way to get the strongest vice is some form of personal sacrifice.
  2. Starting at 4th (3rd?) level the Dark Knight receives the ability to cast certain Mage Spells. The spells they are allowed to receive are those pertaining to Illusion and Necromancy. Additionally the following spells are also available: Darkness 15’ Radius, Wall of Gloom, and Blink.The Dark Knight’s spell progression is similar to that of a bard 1 level lower.The Dark Knight “Unlocks” and receives these spells via Vices - 10 Vices are required to unlock a spell of equal or lesser spell level to the Dark Knight, if the Dark Knight wishes, he may attempt to plead with an additional 5 vices in order to attempt to choose the spell he receives. This is a 5% chance. This chance is augmented by 5% * the Dark Knight’s level. The Dark Knight may also put 5 vices towards locking the spell gain at a certain level. Say he doesn't want any 1st level spells, he can lock it at 2nd level only.

    May become Proficient in: Dagger, Staff, Dart, Sling

    Non-Weapon Profs:

    No Change

    Class Features:

    Unlike mages, they do not gain their power from years of fastidious study and research -- rather, they are born with magic in their blood, and gradually learn to harness their innate abilities over time.

    Sorcerers only learn new spells when they reach certain levels. Otherwise, the spell learning process is a lot different from that of normal mages.

    A Sorcerer may learn another spell as such:

    1.The Sorcerer must see the spell cast by another person.

    2.The Sorcerer must then have a written copy of the spell (Either from a spellbook or scroll) that the Sorcerer is able to read (Via a Read Magic Spell).

    3.The Sorcerer must then make a Know Spell attempt at Half Strength, if someone who knows the spell can actively help the Sorcerer the Know Spell attempt may be made at normal strength.

    4.If Passed, the Sorcerer retains access to the spell. This may allow the Sorcerer to surpass the normal number of spells known. The Sorcerer may only do this process a number of times equal to their max spells per level from int.

    5.Once failed, the Sorcerer may never attempt to learn the spell again, and cannot innately receive it either, it is barred from access.

    *Assassin*:

    Description:  

    A more combat oriented thief.

    Ability Requirements:

                    A Dark Knight must have the following ability scores:

    Strength: 12

    Intelligence: 11

    Dexterity: 12

          Prime Requisites:

    Intelligence: 16

    Dexterity: 16

    Alignment:

                    Any aside from Good

    Armor:

                    Armor like that of a Thief

    Weapon Profs:

    May become proficient in any weapon and in One-handed, Two-Weapon, and missile/thrown fighting style. May not specialize in a weapon.

    Receives 3 Weapon profs at lvl 1

    Non-Weapon Profs:

    Learns NWP as a Thief

    Class Features:

    HD: 1d8/lvl -> +2 HP for every lvl beyond lvl 10

    Thac0 Scaling: As Thief Rebuild

    The Assassin receives the following abilities:

    1. They are able to assassinate as a Thief with any weapon, so long as they prepare an ambush well in advance. This takes a few rounds (determined by the DM) and is dependent on the surrounding terrain.
    2. If the assassin takes time to analyze a specific enemy, they may gain the ability to assassinate non-human opponents. This requires a correct nwp prof or set of nwps in order to learn the weaknesses of a target.EX: An assassin wants to be able to assassinate a bear, the proficiencies of Animal Lore and Butchery must be required. The animal must be slain. A successful butchery check must be made in order to delicately analyze the insides of the bear, and a successful animal lore check must be made to have the information of the bear, after which a successful intelligence check must be made. After all checks have been passed on the bear, the assassin learns the weak points of the specific creature and may then assassinate bears.
    3. The Assassin gets the toxicology prof for free, without needing the prerequisites. However, he may not extract or make poisons from Flora or Fauna without the corresponding proficiencies.
    4. When acquiring thief skills only gains 8 Skill levels at lvl 1, but no more than 6 in any one skill. and 50 Points for each level thereafter.
